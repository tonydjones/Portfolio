TONY D JONES

THE BIOLOGIST

In 2013 Tony started school at MIT, widely regarded as the best university in the world. While at MIT, Tony did research at the Whitehead Institute, researching how cells import amino acids and how cells determine when they have enough resources to begin the process of mitosis. Tony was an author on the paper which was published in the journal Science. Click the image to the right to see his paper.
Tony spent 4 summers working at the Minority Introduction To Engineering and Science (MITES) summer program, in which he worked as the teaching assistant for the Genomics course. He taught high school students how to use experiments, research papers, and their brains to explore the human microbiome.
In 2017 Tony graduated MIT with his bachelor's degree in biology. His spent 2 years working at the Broad Institute, studying the mechanism via which macrophages transform and become malignant in lupus nephritis patients, an auto-immune disorder that destroys a patient's kidneys.
